The TrackPoint

Description

The Trackpoint, also referred to as the 'nub' or 'nipple' is a small 'nub' usually sat between the 'g' 'h' and 'b' keys on a laptop's keyboard, if equipped. It moves the cursor around the screen.

Purpose

The purpose of the Trackpoint is to move the cursor around the screen, requiring less finger movement from the keyboard.

Beneficial Effects

The Trackpoint allows for more productivity by reducing the amount of time required to switch to moving the cursor after typing.

The Trackpoint removes the problem of wrists messing with the trackpad, if used exclusively.

Harmful Effect

A user of the trackpoint may become acquainted to their settings on their own computer, making it harder to switch to a trackpoint on a different computer.

A user who is unable to perform precise finger movements would be unable to use this device.

Impact

The Trackpoint has made an impact on computer users by increasing productivity in situations that require swtiching from typing to moving the mouse cursor. This increased productivity makes a mark on the economy, even though it may be small.

Data Collection

Because of te nature of any pointing device, it needs to collect the movement data in order to move the cursor around the screen. One concerns is that of keyloggers. A keylogger can log mouse movements on a macine, possibly exposing sensitive information.
